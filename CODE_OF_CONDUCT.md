# Fault×32 Code of Conduct

## Our Community

Welcome to the **Fault×32 community**! This project brings together learners, creators, and enthusiasts from all over the world. We are a mix of professionals, volunteers, and curious minds exploring hardware simulation, coding, and in-game experimentation.  

Diversity is our strength, but it can also create misunderstandings. This Code of Conduct (COC) is a **guide to help everyone collaborate, learn, and have fun safely**. It applies to **all spaces managed by Fault×32**, including GitHub, Discord, Roblox forums, and future community platforms.

---

## Be Friendly and Respectful

- Treat others with **respect, patience, and curiosity**.  
- **Harassment, discrimination, or harmful behavior will not be tolerated.**  
- Mild jokes and playful swearing are allowed **if they do not hurt or annoy** anyone.  
- Disagreements are natural. Strive to **understand other perspectives** and keep discussions constructive.  

---

## Inclusion and Safety

- **Do not** discriminate against anyone based on race, gender, nationality, sexual orientation, age, religion, disability, or other personal characteristics.  
- **Do not** post offensive language, dox, share NSFW content, or otherwise harm anyone.  
- In-game “viruses,” hacking, or programmable behavior is allowed **if it remains within the game environment** or communicates with real-world machines, APIs, or endpoints, **but must never harm, exploit, or compromise real-life devices, accounts, or systems**. Any activity that attempts to damage, hack, or exploit external systems is **strictly prohibited**.  
- Illegal or harmful behavior in any space is strictly forbidden.  

---

## Code and Contributions

- All contributions fall under the **Fault×32 Collaborative Source License (FCSL)**. See the [LICENSE](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/uagoose/Fault32/refs/heads/main/LICENSE).  
- Always **credit other contributors** when referencing their work or tutorials.  
- Public forks, clones, or near-copies require **explicit permission from moderators**.  
- Contributors are encouraged to **experiment, learn, and mentor** others while respecting these rules.  

---

## Collaboration and Humor

- Creativity, memes, jokes, and playful experiments are **encouraged**, as long as they **do not harm, expose personal information, or break the rules**.  
- Learning and collaboration are highly valued, but respect the boundaries of the project and other contributors.  

---

## Enforcement

- Moderators are responsible for handling violations.  
- Minor infractions may result in **friendly warnings** and guidance.  
- Repeated or serious violations can lead to **removal from community spaces or project access**.  

---

## Reporting Issues

If you see a violation or feel unsafe:  

- On **Discord**: DM a moderator.  
- On **GitHub**: open an issue flagged for moderator attention.  

We want everyone to enjoy Fault×32 and learn safely. Questions? Reach out — we’ll happily clarify rules or help resolve conflicts.

---

## Friendly Note

Fault×32 is about **exploration, education, and fun**. Follow this code, be kind, credit others, experiment responsibly, and help make the community an exciting place for everyone.
